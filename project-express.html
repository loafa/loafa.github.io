Project Express MIT Media Lab Research Project [Fluid Interfaces]

Project Description

A system to facilitate visual art techniques as a form of expression, "Project Express" is a tool to provide people with muscular disabilities access to painting. As of now, the techniques and technologies that exist have a lot of room for improvement. Without assistive technology, those with limited arm mobility often use a paintbrush held in their mouth. Current mechanical systems, such as a wheelchair attachment or a laser to guide an assistant who traces the laser's motion with paint, could be improved in their expressiveness and their ease of use. Additionally, "Project Express" aims to place control directly into the hands of the artist rather than having them rely on an assistant.

I worked on creating the interface between the data-transmitting device and our airbrush system, setting it up so that others could easily create drivers for a variety of different devices that could seamlessly integrate with the airbrush.

In collaboration with Tal Achituv

Languages Used: Javascript, Arduino, HTML, CSS
